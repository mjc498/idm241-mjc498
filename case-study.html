Case Study

Context and Challenge

Background

My final project for IDM241 focuses on enhancing LinkedIn's "Connect" microinteraction within the Grow Tab under the "Suggested people for you based on your recent activity" section. Conducted as a solo effort, the project was part of a Microinteractions class taught by Professor Jervis Thompson. Over the course of 10 weeks, six assignments culminated in the production of the final project: Alpha - Description, Alpha - Build, Beta - Description, Beta - Build, Final - Description, Final - Build, and this case study. The project operated on a zero-cost budget, aligning with its goal of providing students hands-on experience with modern design theories and front-end scripting languages. This project also fulfilled the class's learning objectives by focusing on the development of meaningful and effective micro-interactions.

The Problem

Modern professional networking tools rely heavily on seamless and intuitive user experiences. Although functional, LinkedIn’s “Connect” feature within the Grow Tab presented opportunities for a more accessible and engaging design. This project aimed to enhance the user experience by making navigation more intuitive and refining the interaction to better align with user needs and expectations. I focused primarily on the "Connect" button, as the original design featured dull visuals and uninspiring interactions across the webpage. I recognized significant potential for improvement in the design concepts within the LinkedIn interface.

Goals & Objectives

By evaluating the triggers, rules, feedback, and loops and modes, these new designs could heavily influence future user experiences. The goal of this project was to create a microinteraction that users would find engaging and satisfying, encouraging them to continue using this LinkedIn feature. Success was defined by designing a smooth and intuitive interaction that felt both practical and enjoyable to use. Accessibility-friendly features and non-abrupt changes were carefully considered throughout the entire process.

Process and Insight

Target Audience

LinkedIn consists of professionals, learners, business owners, students, and more. Recently graduated students and people seeking entry-level job opportunities utizilize LinkedIn (e.g., Jessica Pell in the User Persona below). Creative and ambitious users are also found on this platform to showcase their creativity through strong work ethics and networking. Eager learners utilize the LinkedIn Learning feature which offers certifications in various industries after successful completion of the prospective course. Whether you are looking to show off your skills, grow a business, or gain mentorship, LinkedIn provides opportunities for you to drive that passion for ambition and initiative.

User Persona

Jessica is a recent college graduate entering the digital media industry. She is actively pursuing an entry-level job by leveraging LinkedIn as part of her daily routine. She connects with at least one key industry professional each week, striving to build meaningful relationships in hopes of securing her dream job in social media marketing.

The Solution

Link to Final Build

Container Design

The container serves as the structural backbone of the microinteraction. Its clean, minimalistic design ensures that users can focus on the interactive elements without distraction. The layout is deliberately simple, with adequate spacing around each element to maintain clarity and prevent clutter. The container is fully responsive, adapting to various screen sizes while maintaining the alignment and functionality of its components. The design prioritizes accessibility, ensuring all users can interact with the container effortlessly, whether they are on a desktop or a mobile device.

Connect - Default Design

The "Connect" button in its active state is a central feature of the interaction. The button is visually prominent, with a dark blue background and light blue text that provides excellent contrast, ensuring readability and visibility. The active state conveys a sense of readiness, inviting users to take action. Its design strikes a balance between aesthetic appeal and functionality, making it immediately clear that it is an actionable element. The button's size and padding are optimized for both desktop and mobile usage, ensuring it is touch-friendly on smaller devices.

Connect - Active Design

When the "Connect" button transitions to the pending state, it visually reflects a change in status. The color scheme remains consistent with the active state (light blue text on a dark blue background), but the text shifts to "Pending," providing immediate feedback to the user. This state effectively communicates that the action has been completed and is awaiting further processing, eliminating confusion. The subtlety of the design ensures that the button remains visually cohesive with the rest of the interface while providing necessary status updates.

"X" Design

The "X" button serves as a secondary action for users who wish to dismiss or cancel a connection. Its design is intentionally subtle yet intuitive, with a minimal icon that does not compete for attention with the primary "Connect" button. The button is positioned strategically within the container, ensuring it is easily accessible without disrupting the visual flow. The size and color contrast make it easily identifiable while maintaining a cohesive design language with the rest of the interface.

View Profile Design

The "View Profile" element is designed to enhance user navigation and provide additional functionality. Upon hover, the text "View Profile" appears along the top curvature of the profile picture in white, creating a dynamic and visually appealing interaction. This design choice not only reinforces the functionality of the profile image but also adds a layer of depth and sophistication to the interaction. The placement ensures that it does not interfere with other elements, while the hover state provides a clear indication of interactivity.
